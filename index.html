I work on developer infrastructure — the layer underneath the product, where one good abstraction saves a team a year of work. Eight years of it, mostly backend, mostly on the JVM.

Two projects are worth your time:

Dev & maintainer

Viaduct

Airbnb's open-source data-oriented service mesh. Instead of every team wiring up its own access patterns, Viaduct puts a single highly connected GraphQL schema in front of the entire estate, and services talk to that. I work on the framework components behind service integration, the type-safe GraphQL layer, dependency injection, and the Bazel and Gradle build path.

github.com/airbnb/viaduct → Kotlin · GraphQL · Bazel

Creator

GraphDSL

A Gradle plugin that reads your .graphqls files and generates type-safe Kotlin query builders from them. No hand-written query strings, no separate serialisation layer, and no way to send a query the schema won't accept — the compiler catches it first. Schema changes propagate to every consumer on the next build.

github.com/graphdsl/graphql-kotlin-dsl → Kotlin · Gradle · Codegen
